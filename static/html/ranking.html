Detailed Information

Health Implications

Air quality has direct impacts on public health. According to the World Health Organization, air pollution is responsible for approximately 7 million premature deaths globally each year. The risk of stroke, heart disease, lung cancer, and both chronic and acute respiratory diseases increases with exposure to poor air quality.

Countries with lower AQI values provide healthier living environments with reduced risk of pollution-related health conditions. Those with higher values face significant public health challenges that require targeted interventions and policy changes.
